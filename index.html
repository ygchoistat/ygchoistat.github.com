I am a postdoctoral fellow in the Department of Statistics at Seoul National University. My research interests broadly lie on unsupervised learning from data and public health studies. During the (post-)doctoral training, I focused on inferences on large-scale covariance matrices, longitudinal data analysis, and survival analysis. My thesis deals with positive definiteness of structured covariance matrix estimation and its application for rehabilitative speech treatment for Parkinson's disease patients and Markowitz's portfolio optimization problem. I also have involved in research on random matrix theory, cohort study, electronic medical record, and analysis of mass-spectrometry data.
